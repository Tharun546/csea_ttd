Pakhal Lake

This breathtaking lake is located approximately 200 km from Hyderabad and is one of the most popular ones in the state. It is a man-made lake which was built during the era of Kakatiya King Ganapati Deva as a source of water for irrigation purpose. This lake is spread across 30 sq. km amidst flourishing greenery, forests and hills. This is a popular go-to picnic spot because of its serenity and beauty. For nature enthusiasts, this place is an ideal spot to relax and recharge. You can hear the sounds of birds chirping, cold breeze and get to enjoy a few tranquil moment

Mallela Theertham Waterfalls

If you reside in Hyderabad and are searching for waterfalls that are close by then Mallela Theertham has to be one! This charming and serene waterfall is at a distance of 58 km from Srisailam and 173 km from Hyderabad. This waterfall has a height of 150 ft that gushes over the rocks. It is bordered by a thick forest offering scenic views, thus giving an unforgettable experience to its visitors. It is also admired and favored by students and working professionals as it is closer to Hyderabad. Plan to visit this weekend and enjoy the view of water surging and plunging down the huge rocks.

Bogatha Waterfalls

Situated in the forest region at a distance of 30 km from Eturnagaram and 274 km from Hyderabad, Bogatha Waterfalls might seem very distant but it is worth the visit! For the nearby villagers, this cataract is a go-to spot for any fun-filled activities like picnic or swimming. It stands 30 ft tall but is voluminous and swishes down the basalt rock into a large pool at the bottom of the rock. Since it is considered safe and sound by the local residents, you can plan a small trip and taste the adventure.

Palair Lake

The lake is at a distance of about 30 kilometers from the main city and can be accessed easily by road. Thought the lake is man-made, we can find it equally beautiful like a natural lake. It is actually a balancing reservoir of the Lal Bahadur Canal, which was built as a part of the Nagarjunasagar project. The lake has been constructed over an area of approximately 1748 hectares and it has a capacity of storing close to 2.5 TMC water. The water from the Palair Lake is used to serve irrigational purposes, and it is a quiet famous spot for fish farming. The Lake is a very popular tourist attraction of the Khammam city because it provides both water sports as well as some adrenaline adventure activities. The Palair Lake also has a huge range of prawns and fresh water fish which are not only cultivated here, but are also prepared as mouth melting delicacies for the tourists.

Gayathri Waterfalls

Another magnificent and fascinating waterfalls situated at a distance of 59 km from Adilabad and 270 km away from Hyderabad is the Gayathri Waterfalls. Gayathri Waterfalls pours down the cliff in one cascade from a height of 100 ft into the valley to create a magnificent sight. There is a pool under a waterfall where tourists can swim. The best time to enjoy the splashing water and thunderous roar of the Gayathri Waterfalls is during the peak monsoon.
